Lunar New Year Infographic

Educating students about the cultural traditions in South Korea.

Context

The infographic was a collaboration with SFU Student Engagement & Retention, who wanted to host an event promoting several asian cultures in conjunction with an on-campus Lunar New Years celebration.

Timeline

January 2022 - February 2022

Team

Collaboration with executives at K.Storm, as well as SFU Student Engagement & Retention

Contribution

  • Graphic Design
  • Illustration

Development

Our school club, SFU K.Storm, was approached by an event coordinator from SFU Student Engagement & Retention. SFU K.Storm, being a non-profit student organization that promotes Korean culture, found the event as an opportunity to share and inform students of the Lunar New Year's traditions in Korea.

Due to the fact that our poster would be displayed alongside posters of asian countries, I wanted to create something that looked distinctively Korean so that it would stand out even more.

I began by researching traditions, food, activities, as well as anything that Koreans might celebrate during Lunar New Years. For my colour scheme, I referenced "Obangsaek" (오방색), the traditional Korean colour scheme which uses white, black, red, blue, yellow. Not only were the colours associated with Korean culture and history, but the bright nature of these colours made them the perfect colour scheme for our event.

Afterwards, I began creating illustrations for the infographic. For one of the illustrations, I used K.Storm's mascot, Dubu, to depict the traditional attire (called "hanbok") typically worn during Lunar New Years. Not only was Dubu an adorable mascot, but the korean tiger is one of the national Korean animals that has deep connections to Korean culture and symbolism. In addition, 2022 is the year of the tiger, making the illustration even more fitting.

Results

Upon completion, the poster design was met with immediate approval from K.Storm executives and SFU Student Engagement & Retention. The poster was later posted on a 4ft x 4ft bulletin board for students on campus to read and learn more about Lunar New Year traditions in Korea.
