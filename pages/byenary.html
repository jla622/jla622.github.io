Development

Understanding our Domain

We conducted interviews—both online and in person—with several individuals who identify as part of the LGBTQIA+ community. In our first round of interviews, we interviewed the following people:

  • 3 bisexual individuals
  • 2 gay men
  • 1 trans femme
  • 1 nonbinary individual

Pain Points

After we have conducted the interviews, we analyzed some common patterns that have arised in our interviewee's answers.

  • Many had to unlearn internalized homophobia that was enforced by society.
  • There was very little exposure to the LGBTQIA+ community while growing up.
  • Not much is known local queer history or culture, as most of it is centered around the US.

Ideation

Initial Wireframes

In order to have a visual understanding of our app's interactions, our team designed a low-fidelity wireflow. This included key interactions such as the onboarding process, the main features within the app, as well as avatar customization.

Final Prototype

After multiple iterations with our app development, we created an app prototype that teaches users about the local LGBTQIA+ community in Vancouver, as well as an educational feature that teaches users queer terms and vocabulary. We implemented gamification features in order to make the experience more positive and reassuring, such as adding rewards to the end of each module, such as an accessory. By adding rewards, the user is incentivized to seek out the features within the app.

Challenges

We had two main challenges throughout our entire design process: how do we create a positive learning experience, and how do we create an app designed for LGBTQIA+ users, without assumptions or biases?

To avoid reinforcing any stereotypes about the community, we consulted linguistic experts and LGBTQIA individuals throughout the entire process, from the first steps of research to the final presentation stage.

Reflection

As a student designer, this was one of the biggest interaction design projects that I have led. Not only was I the one managing the entire team to ensure a balanced team dynamic, but I also had to be heavily involved every aspect of designing and prototyping our application.

Despite the challenges involving team leadership and management, the project was an overall success; we received keen interest from industry professionals who reviewed our app, as well as LGBTQIA+ individuals who wanted to see how the potential app might personally benefit them.
